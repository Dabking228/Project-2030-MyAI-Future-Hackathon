# Malaysian Transit Interchange Guides

This document describes walking transfer routes between transit stations
that are physically close but not directly connected by a paid link.
Use this to generate accurate interchange instructions in route responses.

---

## How to Use This Document

When a route requires transferring between two different lines or operators
at the same location, use the walking time and exit information below to
generate the instruction. Always mention the specific exit number or landmark.

---

## KL Sentral (Major Hub — All Lines)

KL Sentral is Malaysia's largest transit hub. Multiple operators share the
building but use different platforms and levels.

### KTM Komuter ↔ KTM ETS / Intercity
- Same building, different platforms (ground floor vs upper floor)
- Walking time: 3–5 minutes
- No re-ticketing required if you have a through ticket

### KTM Komuter ↔ LRT Kelana Jaya Line
- Exit KTM towards the NU Sentral mall connector bridge
- Follow signs to "LRT / Rapid KL"
- Walking time: 5–7 minutes (covered walkway)
- Re-tap required — separate fare

### KTM Komuter ↔ KL Monorail (KL Sentral station)
- Exit KTM main concourse, turn left towards Brickfields
- Monorail platform is on the elevated bridge outside
- Walking time: 4–6 minutes
- Re-tap required — separate fare

### KTM / LRT ↔ ERL (KLIA Ekspres / KLIA Transit)
- ERL departure hall is on Level 1 of the main KL Sentral tower
- Follow "KLIA Ekspres" signage from any platform
- Walking time: 5–8 minutes
- Separate ticket purchase required at ERL counters or kiosks

### KL Sentral ↔ Bus Terminal (Rapid KL / GoKL)
- Exit via the NU Sentral street level (Jalan Tun Sambanthan side)
- Bus stops are on the street directly outside
- Walking time: 2–3 minutes

## Masjid Jamek (LRT Kelana Jaya ↔ LRT Ampang/Sri Petaling)

- This is a free transfer station within the Prasarana network
- Tap out of one line and tap into the other using the same MyRapid card
- Within 30 minutes: no additional boarding charge
- Both platforms are in the same station complex
- Transfer concourse: follow overhead signs for "Ampang Line" or "Kelana Jaya Line"
- Walking time between platforms: 2–4 minutes
- Note: The station is split across two levels — Kelana Jaya on upper, Ampang/Sri Petaling on lower

---

## Chan Sow Lin (LRT Sri Petaling ↔ MRT Putrajaya Line)

- Free transfer within Prasarana network (same compound)
- Tap out LRT, walk across the covered bridge, tap into MRT
- Transfer time: 3–5 minutes
- Follow "MRT" or "LRT" overhead signage clearly visible from both platforms
- This interchange connects the LRT network to the MRT Putrajaya Line (V3)

---

## Titiwangsa (LRT Kelana Jaya ↔ LRT Ampang ↔ KL Monorail)

- Three lines meet at Titiwangsa but not in a single integrated station
- LRT Kelana Jaya and LRT Ampang/Sri Petaling: covered walkway, 3–5 min
- LRT ↔ Monorail: Exit LRT station, walk along Jalan Tun Razak, ~5–7 minutes
- All three require re-tapping (separate fares apply)
- Titiwangsa is also a GoKL bus hub — stops outside the LRT exit

---

## Pasar Seni (LRT Kelana Jaya ↔ LRT Ampang / GoKL / Bus)

- LRT Kelana Jaya and Ampang lines share the same station building
- Free Prasarana transfer within 30 minutes (tap in/out)
- Central Market (Pasar Seni) is directly connected via pedestrian walkway
- GoKL buses stop outside on Jalan Hang Kasturi
- Walking time to Petaling Street (Chinatown): 5–8 minutes on foot

---

## Bukit Bintang (Monorail ↔ MRT Kajang Line)

- Not in the same building — underground walkway connects them
- From Monorail Bukit Bintang: take the underground pedestrian tunnel
  signed "MRT Bukit Bintang" (near Lot 10 shopping centre)
- Walking time: 4–6 minutes (air-conditioned tunnel)
- Re-tap required (separate fare for MRT)
- This interchange is heavily used — expect congestion during peak hours

---

## Dang Wangi (LRT Kelana Jaya ↔ MRT Kajang Line — Bukit Nanas)

- LRT Dang Wangi and MRT Bukit Nanas are separate stations ~400m apart
- Exit Dang Wangi LRT via Exit A, walk along Jalan Ampang/Jalan P Ramlee
- Follow pedestrian signs to MRT Bukit Nanas
- Walking time: 6–8 minutes (outdoor, covered section near KL Tower)
- Re-tap required

---

## Subang Jaya (KTM Komuter ↔ LRT Kelana Jaya — USJ stations)

- No direct interchange — nearest LRT stations are ~2–3km away
- KTM Subang Jaya serves the city centre
- LRT Kelana Jaya serves USJ and Subang suburbs (different area)
- Use connecting Rapid KL bus or e-hailing for transfer
- Recommend checking bus routes S412, T411 for connection

---

## Terminal Bersepadu Selatan / Bandar Tasik Selatan (KTM ↔ MRT ↔ Bus)

- KTM Komuter "Bandar Tasik Selatan" and MRT Kajang Line "TBS" are connected
- Free covered walkway between the two (3–5 minutes)
- TBS Bus Terminal is integrated — major long-distance bus hub
- Walking from MRT to bus terminal: 2–3 minutes (Level 1 connection)
- This is the main hub for buses to southern Malaysia, Johor, Singapore

---

## Johor Bahru (JB Sentral Hub)

### JB Sentral ↔ Singapore (Woodlands via bus)
- CIQ (Customs Immigration Quarantine) is inside JB Sentral
- Causeway Link and other express buses depart from Level 2
- Walking from JB Sentral KTM to bus boarding: 3–5 minutes (same building)

### JB Sentral ↔ Local BAS.MY buses
- Local buses depart from the street-level bus stops adjacent to JB Sentral
- Walking time: 2–3 minutes from KTM platform

---

## Ipoh (Ipoh Railway Station ↔ Local Bus Terminal)

- Ipoh Railway Station (KTM ETS/Komuter) is 200m from Medan Kidd bus terminal
- Walking time: 3–5 minutes (outdoor, partially sheltered)
- Local Perak Transit buses connect from Medan Kidd to city and suburbs

---

## General Transfer Tips (include in all interchange instructions)

- Always follow overhead directional signage — Malaysian transit stations
  are generally well-signed in English and Malay
- MyRapid card transfers between Prasarana services (LRT/MRT/Monorail/RapidKL bus)
  are free if done within 30 minutes of tapping out
- KTM and Prasarana are separate systems — always re-tap and pay again
- Keep your MyRapid card balance above RM 2.00 before transferring at major hubs
- Peak hours (7–9 AM, 5–7 PM weekdays) add 2–5 minutes to all transfer times
  due to crowd congestion at escalators and corridors
